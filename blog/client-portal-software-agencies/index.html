The Best Client Portal Software for Agencies in 2026

Lyniro Team · March 25, 2026 · 6 min read

Agencies have a specific problem: managing multiple external clients simultaneously, each at a different stage of a project, with different stakeholders and different definitions of done. Here are the tools built for exactly that.

What agencies need from client portal software

Agency client portals need to do three things well: give clients visibility into project progress without overwhelming them, make it easy for clients to complete their part of the work, and give agency account managers a single view across all active accounts. Most tools built for SaaS CS teams do the first two well. Lyniro is one of the few that also does the third.

Lyniro — Best for structured client onboarding and delivery

Lyniro was built for exactly the agency use case — structured plans with stage-locked progression, email-first task completion so clients act without logging in, and a unified dashboard showing every account's progress and risk level. Built-in chat keeps every client conversation attached to the plan. From $79/month.

Basecamp — Simplest for long-running projects

Basecamp is client-friendly and has been used by agencies for over a decade. It lacks onboarding-specific structure — no stage locking, no at-risk signals — but it is easy for non-technical clients to adopt. Best for agencies with simple, long-running project relationships.

Notion — Most flexible, most setup required

Notion works as a client portal with enough configuration. Many agency teams build elaborate Notion setups only to find they spend more time maintaining the system than using it. Works well for 1–5 clients. Breaks down at 20+.

ClickUp — Best for larger agency operations

ClickUp is a full project management platform that can serve as a client portal. It has more features than most agencies need, but the granular task management and time tracking make it worth considering for agencies with complex delivery processes and internal billing requirements.
