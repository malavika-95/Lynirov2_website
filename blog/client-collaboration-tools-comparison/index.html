Client Onboarding Software Compared — Lyniro vs Dock vs Arrows vs Notion (2026)

Lyniro Team · March 12, 2026 · 7 min read

There are dozens of tools claiming to solve client onboarding. Most are built for adjacent problems — sales rooms, project management, or internal task tracking. Here is an honest comparison of the tools actually built for CS teams.

What to look for in client onboarding software

The best client onboarding software is the one your clients actually use. That means minimising login friction, making tasks obvious, and giving clients visibility into their own progress. On the team side, you need per-CSM analytics, at-risk detection, and a way to maintain internal context that clients never see.

Lyniro — Built for CS teams and structured onboarding

Email-first task completion (clients act from inbox, no login), at-risk detection (automatic flagging of stalled and blocked accounts), built-in chat (no Slack required), internal notes with visibility control, role-based access. Works with any CRM via API. From $79/month. Currently in early access.

Dock — Built for enterprise sales rooms

Polished client workspace. Strong document sharing and embeds. Primarily designed for sales teams managing enterprise deals — not CS teams running 30-day SaaS onboarding. No built-in chat. No email-first completion. No at-risk detection. Starts at $350/month.

Arrows — Built for HubSpot-native teams

Deep HubSpot integration with onboarding progress syncing to deal records. If your CS team lives entirely in HubSpot, it is the best-fit option. If you use any other CRM — Salesforce, Zoho, Pipedrive — the value drops significantly. Starts at approximately $500/month.

Notion — Most flexible, least structured

Infinitely customisable but requires significant setup to function as a client onboarding platform. Works well at small scale. No at-risk detection, no email-first completion, no stage locking out of the box. Best used as a starting point before moving to a dedicated tool.

The honest verdict

For CS teams running structured SaaS onboarding: Lyniro. For enterprise sales rooms with large deal cycles: Dock. For HubSpot-native CS teams: Arrows. For teams with fewer than 5 clients who want to start cheap: Notion. The deciding factor is not features — it is whether your clients will actually use it.
